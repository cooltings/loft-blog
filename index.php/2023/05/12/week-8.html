Week 8

Hitting the Gas

After the Coronation Bank Holiday weekend, the gas engineer from SGN came to check out the gas pipe and declared it dead but … oops it wasn’t quite so. Soon gas started spurting out at full speed!!! Enter the emergency team rushing in. They capped and cut the infamous pipe and we were left with no gas – yet they gifted us with electric hob and heaters to keep us going. The cold showers were certainly “refreshing” but by Wednesday night we were all up and running again and it wasn’t so bad in the end. However, our neighbour Judy was left disconnected and it took a whole day of phone calls to get the team to come back and sort her out on Friday. 

In the meantime, our builders were growing impatient, as they had the go-ahead from Building Control and were eager to pour the cement down for the foundations of our front extension.

Whilst waiting for the gas pipes to be sorted, they got busy with odd indoors jobs. All the doors were hung, architraves cut, handles and locks installed, fire strips stuck in, push doors insulated and installed, skirting boards nailed in place and Jake even managed to square up our bathroom door as best as he could. 

Nick bounced back as good as new. Some of the stair rails were built and our front door ordered.

On Tuesday, Dave the plumber came for his first fix: he installed the shower tray and the vanity unit and connected the radiators and the sink. Luckily we had managed to paint behind the radiators over the weekend but I guess we’ll never get to do that second coat….

As soon as Jake hung the wall cabinet in the bathroom, Tim the electrician installed a toothbrush recharger inside it. Genius.

Tim also managed his first fix, connecting the extractor fan, the plugs and the down lights but Anthony was very disappointed because nothing was smart despite our specific requests. Hopefully, it’ll get fixed when he comes back.

The loft is looking more and more like a room now and we can’t wait to move in. The bad news is that the decorator is no longer available so we will be painting forevermore.

On Friday, Jake started cutting the bamboo flooring for the first floor landing and put it in place ready to be glued down. The new bamboo delivery arrived too and was transferred to the loft to acclimatise.

By lunchtime on Friday, our team was finally able to lay the foundations so they can rest over the weekend. Lunch this time had to be a quick bite standing for our builders but the job got done and there was birthday cake to make up for it. Angie had to walk the plank several times (very scary – the floor is worse than lava!) but didn’t end up stuck in the wet cement so all is good.

Well, not the ideal week for Luca’s SATs exams and Anthony’s birthday but now the project upstairs is really coming to an end. Next week the tiler will come and then Dave will do his second fix whilst half of our building team will tackle the bamboo floor. By the end of next week we might even be able to move in – at least with the bed – if we paint the ceilings over this weekend. The rest of the team will concentrate on the front extension and we should be all done in three weeks or so.
