Week 5

We are now mid-April and this week has been really key to our project. On Monday, the vertical windows for the back of the dormer went in. Unluckily, the bedroom one has a broken pane but it will get sorted in due time. At the same time, the staircase arrived and they started building it in the study room, a bit like a 3D jigsaw. Insulation started being fitted in the ceiling and stud walls. All the material was taken up to the loft and the drive became nearly free again.

Tuesday was the wow day. The staircase was built and glued into place and we were allowed to go up for the first time. What a stunning view over the the park we have from up there! Two of the veluxes at the front of the house were put in and the roof tiles we restored. We were not supposed to go up to the loft in the evening while the glue was setting but dad could not resist a peek when he came back from work. Luckily, no damage done.

By Wednesday, the staircase had been bolted to the party wall and to the frame of the study studwall, which had been built. The third velux (the one over the stairwell) had been installed, flooding the stairs with lovely natural light. Most of the insulation had gone in and some plasterboards had started to get nailed down to the ceiling and walls. What a difference it makes! The loft is starting to look more and more like a room and less and less like a building site.

Thursday was a goregeous day but unfortunately the flat roofers weren’t free, so the bathroom velux had to wait. Our builders did not waste any time though. They built the little doors for the wall towards the eaves, put almost all the insulation on and got on with installing plasterboards everywhere, inlcuding under the stairs. We even managed to catch a breathtaking sunset from the bathroom window.

Friday was forecast for rain but it held off till midday, giving the flat roofer enough time to get started. The flat roof is on, the bathroom window is on and nearly all the plasterboards are in place – you can see the pile of plasterboards in the middle of the room has really gone down. Some water is still dripping down from the velux as it got quite wet at some point and water accumulated on the flat roof. With a blow torch, however, the roof was quickly dried and sealed.

Next week, we’ll be a bit weather-dependent for cementing the roof ridge and sides and installing the cladding. If it is really wet, they’ll just finish off the “inside jobs”: plasterboards, final insulation, door trimmings and window sills.

Jake forgot to move the plug to make room for mummy’s wardrobe in the corner but no worries – Luke has put a nice reminder for him for Monday.
